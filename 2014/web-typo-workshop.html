Dave and Pathum

Dave Crossland is a font consultant from England working with Google Fonts. Dave participates in the FontForge project and offers lectures on type design around the world with Crafting Type.

Pathum Egodawatta is a graphic and type designer from Sri Lanka, working with Dave to improve access to font design education, tools and culture in developing countries.

Workshop: Web Typography For The Next Billion People

Typography can seem like a dark art, hidden in mystery. While good typography is essential to good design, most designers do not know that web typography can now be as good as in anything in print.

As the next billion people join the internet, many will be Indian citizens who are not literate in English. Their primary internet devices will be mobiles or tablets, which makes typography critical because it fills their small screens entirely for most of the time.

In this workshop you will learn why typography is important, how to work with web fonts (including some development details that anyone can master) and you’ll have plenty of time to ask any questions you might have.

Dave Crossland and Pathum Egodawatta will optionally review your existing web typography and ensure that each person leaves feeling confident when choosing and using fonts on the web, for both publications and applications.

Beginners welcome!

    Target Audience:
  1. Any who wants to improve their typography design
  2. Web and Graphic Designers
  3. Advertisers, Marketers, Authors, and other Communications Professionals
  4. Bloggers, News publishers, Magazine editors
  5. Students, Professionals, Educators

    Agenda:
  1. Why Typography Matters
  2. Examples of Good and Bad Typography
  3. What is Web Typography
  4. Typography for the Mobile Web
  5. How Web Fonts Work
  6. Why Web Font Services Help
  7. Advert Typography Experiment
  8. Blog Typography Experiment
  9. Web Typography For Applications, Advertisements and Publications
  10. Review of your previous works
